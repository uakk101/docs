A patient record in CodeCrafting Hub is the central object around which all clinical and financial activity is organized. Every encounter, appointment, insurance policy, and claim ties back to a patient, so understanding the patient data model — including how identifiers work and how status changes are governed — is foundational to building reliable integrations on the platform.

## What a Patient Record Contains

Patient records store two categories of information: **demographics** used to identify and contact the individual, and **administrative data** used to manage their relationship with your organization.

### Demographics

| Field | Description | Required |
|---|---|---|
| `first_name` | Legal first name | Yes |
| `last_name` | Legal last name | Yes |
| `date_of_birth` | ISO 8601 date (`YYYY-MM-DD`) | Yes |
| `gender` | `male`, `female`, `other`, `unknown` | Yes |
| `address` | Structured address object (see below) | No |
| `phone` | Primary contact phone number | No |
| `email` | Primary contact email address | No |

<Note>
  The `gender` field represents administrative gender as defined by HL7 FHIR R4 and is distinct from a patient's clinical gender identity or sex assigned at birth. Use the `extensions` object to record additional gender-related clinical data.
</Note>

### Insurance Information

You can attach one or more insurance policies to a patient record via the **Insurance Policies** sub-resource (`/patients/{id}/insurance-policies`). Each policy stores the payer ID, member ID, group number, relationship to the subscriber, and coverage effective dates. The platform uses the policy with the highest priority (`sequence: 1`) as primary insurance during claim generation.

## Patient Identifiers

Every patient has three distinct identifiers, each serving a different purpose:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Platform ID (id)">
    **Field:** `id`  
    **Prefix:** `pat_`  
    **Example:** `pat_01HX9Z2K3M4N5P6Q7R8S9T0U`

    The platform's canonical, immutable identifier for a patient. This ID is generated by CodeCrafting Hub at the time of patient creation and never changes — even if the patient record is merged or deactivated. Use `id` as your primary reference in API calls and webhooks.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Medical Record Number (mrn)">
    **Field:** `mrn`  
    **Prefix:** `MRN-`  
    **Example:** `MRN-00042`

    A human-readable Medical Record Number auto-generated by the platform and formatted for clinical staff. MRNs are sequentially assigned per organization and guaranteed unique within your organization. You cannot set or change an MRN — the platform manages this value entirely.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="External ID (external_id)">
    **Field:** `external_id`  
    **Example:** `EHR-PAT-9871`

    An optional, user-supplied identifier that maps the platform patient back to a record in your own system (e.g., a legacy EHR, practice management system, or data warehouse). The `external_id` must be unique within your organization. You can query patients by `external_id` using `GET /patients?external_id=EHR-PAT-9871`.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Always populate `external_id` during patient creation if you are integrating with an existing system. It dramatically simplifies reconciliation between CodeCrafting Hub and your source-of-truth database without requiring you to store platform IDs externally.
</Tip>

## Patient Status

Every patient record carries a `status` field that governs how the record can be used in clinical and billing workflows.

<Steps>
  <Step title="active">
    The default status for a newly created patient. Active patients can be scheduled for appointments, linked to encounters, and billed against. This is the expected state for the majority of your patient population.
  </Step>
  <Step title="inactive">
    The patient is no longer receiving care at your organization but their record is retained for historical and compliance purposes. Inactive patients cannot be linked to new encounters or appointments, but existing records remain queryable and billable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="deceased">
    Marks the patient as deceased. Set the `deceased_date` field alongside this status. Deceased patients are excluded from appointment scheduling and outreach workflows. Existing claims and billing obligations are not affected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  You cannot permanently delete a patient record through the API. Deletion would break referential integrity across encounters, claims, and audit logs. To stop a record from appearing in active workflows, set its status to `inactive` or `deceased`.
</Warning>

## Privacy, Merging & Audit Logging

CodeCrafting Hub enforces strict privacy controls on all patient data in alignment with HIPAA requirements.

**Record Merging:** When duplicate patient records exist, you can merge them using the `POST /patients/{id}/merge` endpoint. You designate a *winner* (the surviving record) and a *loser* (the record to be absorbed). All encounters, claims, and insurance policies associated with the loser are re-linked to the winner. The loser record is retained in a `merged` state and queryable via its original ID — it is never destroyed.

**Deactivation:** Setting a patient's status to `inactive` immediately removes them from all active scheduling and outreach queues. The record and all associated clinical and financial data remain intact and accessible to authorized users.

**Audit Logging:** Every create, update, merge, and status change on a patient record is immutably written to the platform's audit log. Audit events include the timestamp, the user or API key that made the change, the previous value, and the new value. You can retrieve the full audit trail for a patient at `GET /patients/{id}/audit-logs`.

<Info>
  Audit logs are retained for a minimum of seven years to meet HIPAA record retention requirements. Logs cannot be modified or deleted through any API endpoint.
</Info>
